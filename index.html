Abstract

We present CrossSDF, a novel approach for reconstructing a 3D signed-distance field from 2D cross-sections. Reconstructing complex structures from planar cross-sections is a challenging problem, with wide-reaching applications in medical imaging, manufacturing, and topography. Out-of-the-box point cloud reconstruction methods can often fail due to the data sparsity between slicing planes, while current bespoke methods struggle to reconstruct thin geometric structures and preserve topological continuity. This is important for medical applications where thin vessel structures are present in CT and MRI scans.

This paper introduces CrossSDF, a novel approach for extracting a 3D signed distance field from 2D signed distances generated from planar contours. Our approach makes the training of neural SDFs contour-aware by using losses designed for the case where geometry is known within 2D slices. Our results demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods, effectively reconstructing thin structures and producing accurate 3D models without the interpolation artifacts or over-smoothing of prior approaches.

From 2D Signed Distance Function to Neural 3D SDF Representation

Our pipeline transforms 2D signed distance functions from cross-sectional data into a complete 3D neural SDF representation. The process involves careful encoding of spatial information, adaptive sampling strategies, and our novel loss functions to ensure accurate reconstruction of thin structures while maintaining topological consistency between slices.

Adaptive Sampling and Hybrid Encoding

We introduce an adaptive sampling strategy that focuses on regions where thin structures are present, ensuring detailed reconstruction. A hybrid encoding strategy combines hash-grid and Fourier features to capture fine details while maintaining surface smoothness.
